Indeed, popular are cautionary tales that expound the perils of the night; the perils of the shadows; the perils of the figures that lurk in the before two;

Those figures that stalk thier victims as they walk home, to school, or to none of the above so as to conceal any location to which the victim daily retires.

Those creeps. Those criminals. Those thieves. Those predators. Those perverts. Those... people. Those... humans.

But who dare not empathize with their victims, whom through a kitchen sink of tactics avoid victimhood?

Those with whom no one, neither initiated woman nor initiated man, ever though to empathize: the uninitiated, innocent man.

Initiated women's earbuds seem to come loose in front of them.

Initiated men's shoe laces seem to unravel in front of them.

Initiated women's feet seem to tire ... and slow down in front of them.

Initiated men's phones prompt a sharp left turn ... to the other side of the road in front of them.

We all, initiated and uninitiated, know too well the consummate fear that takes hold when one of them approaches from behind. You've read the stories. The fear is inevitable.
