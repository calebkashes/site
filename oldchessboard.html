(03/04/2025) In 200 words: Tariff Tuesday

Let’s forget China for a moment. Today, March 4th, a preliminary round of tariffs hit imports from Mexico and Canada.

President Trump floats this executive power to enact tariffs as an ace up his sleeve — a trump card, if you will — in a concerted effort to advance his foremost policy goal of border protection. What a few months ago seemed to be campaign noise is now a strong signal.

I’ve been alarmed by the nonchalance of many Wall Street strategists in light of this. Optimism and market sentiment led analysts to ignore the fact that the S&P 500 traded at a 25x multiple in mid-December — the median year-end forecast for the index was 6,600 just before January's inauguration.

The recent turbulence doesn’t invalidate those year-end estimates, however from my meager vantage point, the realization of President Trump’s economic policies was a stumbling block too tall to overlook, and yet the market has.

Beyond the tariff fallout and retaliation, I imagine the resolution of the Russia-Ukraine war will dictate any broad market movement in the week to come, i.e. for better or worse, views on the United States and its relation to world order will take center stage.
